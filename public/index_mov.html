Toy Story 2

Summary

"Toy Story 2">Toy Story 2" is a 1999 animated film produced by Pixar Animation Studios and released by
Walt Disney Pictures. The movie continues the story of Woody, Buzz Lightyear, and the other
toys from Andy's room. In this sequel, Woody is accidentally placed on a yard sale by his owner,
Andy's mother. Woody is stolen by a toy collector named Al McWhiggin.

Buzz Lightyear and the other toys embark on a mission to rescue Woody. Along the way, they encounter
new characters like Jessie the cowgirl, Bullseye the horse, and Stinky Pete the prospector.
The toys also learn that Woody is a valuable collectible from a 1950s television show called
"Woody's Roundup."

The story explores themes of friendship, loyalty, and the value of toys in a child's life.
Ultimately, Woody must decide between returning to Andy or joining a museum exhibit in Japan, and
the toys work together to overcome various obstacles and challenges.

Opinion

I feel the world was set up from the first one, and therefore, there was space to explore the characters more.


Lord of the Rings: Fellowship of the Ring

Summary

"The Fellowship of the Ring" is the first installment in the film adaptation of J.R.R. Tolkien's
"The Lord of the Rings" trilogy, directed by Peter Jackson.

The story begins in the peaceful Shire, where Frodo Baggins inherits a mysterious and powerful
ring from his uncle Bilbo. The wizard Gandalf reveals that the ring is the One Ring, forged by
the dark lord Sauron to control all other rings and dominate Middle-earth.
Frodo, along with his friends Sam, Merry, and Pippin, sets out on a perilous journey to destroy
the ring and prevent Sauron's return to power.

"The Fellowship of the Ring" sets the stage for the epic quest to destroy the One Ring and
confront the growing threat of Sauron. The film combines stunning visuals, memorable characters,
and a captivating storyline to bring Tolkien's fantasy world to life.

Opinion

This was great entrance into an otherwise unknown world.


Tenet

Summary

"Tenet" is a 2020 science fiction thriller film written and directed by Christopher Nolan.
The movie follows the protagonist, known simply as "The Protagonist," played by John David Washington,
as he navigates a world of international espionage with a unique twist involving the manipulation of time.

The central concept in "Tenet" is the existence of inverted objects and people,
which can move backward through time. The Protagonist is recruited into an organization
called Tenet and embarks on a mission to prevent a global catastrophe orchestrated by
a mysterious Russian arms dealer named Andrei Sator, portrayed by Kenneth Branagh.
Sator possesses technology that allows him to invert time, posing a significant threat to the world.

The film explores themes of free will, determinism, and the consequences of manipulating time.
With its intricate plot, impressive practical effects, and Nolan's signature visual style,
"Tenet" challenges viewers to engage with its complex narrative and unravel the mysteries
of its temporal structure.

Opinion

I love the concept of time travel, and this movie did it in a way that was unique and interesting.
